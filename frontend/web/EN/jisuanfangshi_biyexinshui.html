Colleges With Highest Salaries After Graduation

1. The data source

The rankings are calculated based on the earnings of alumni released by the Bureau of Education each year. A total of 3,126 universities were included.

2. How we calculate the salaries after graduation

The rankings are based on the median salaries of the alumni 6 years after graduation. Due to the family financial situation difference among students, the data only takes into account the students who receive federal student aid. The actual average earnings should be higher than the information showed in the table.

Some students choose to attend graduate school (a master degree usually takes about 2 years while the PhD degree takes for about 5 years) after graduated from college. Therefore, for top Ph.D. feeder colleges, the earnings look lower because many students are still in the process of completing their Ph.D. degree. Schools like Caltech, MIT and Harvey Mudd College should have a higher statistics in reality.
